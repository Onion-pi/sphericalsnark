Advice for College

  • When in doubt, refer to a professor, regardless of gender, as "Mrs. [Name]."
  • If you forget the name of a professor, simply refer to them as "Mrs. Nameless"
  • Being excited about a topic is the worst way to learn.
  • Sleep if for the weak. Breaks and proper sleeping schedules only hinder you.
  • If you believe you would enjoy a diverse college, go to Princeton.
  • Ensure that you nclude the results of all math competitions you have ever participated in on your application.
  • This is especially true for very small regional ones.
  • This includes competitions you performed poorly in. Simply preface your result with "there were many participants"
  • Go to MIT for your Undergraduate Mathematics degree.
  • Ensure that your email address is as long and unprofessional as possible.
  • It is completely acceptable to ask Professors about their syllabus and other easily found information.
  • Deadlines are always optional. Extensions are rarely provided.
  • There is only one college that is perfect for every student: North Eastern Federal University.
  • Do what you think will get you a job. Your passions will get you nowhere.
  • The only mathematical job is teaching.
