I am currently the Director of MathIGy, a Research Associate and high-school teacher at Kram Yentaw, and an instructor at the Art of Painful Repitition

Some years back, my parents told me I had to go into mathematical research. While I started in the typical places, such as MathILy, I eventually realized that everything there was much too fun. Instead, I began to carefully research what subjects were the regarded as the most painful by students. One day, I was walking home from school in Siberia when I found a book called Differential Geometry by Erwin Kreyszig. It was the most tedious, boring, pointless book I had ever read, and I did not understand a word of it. That's when I knew I had to go into differential geometry. Interesting mathematics is too cliche.

Steps for Doing Mathematics:
  1. Learn a formula, preferably by rote, without any knowledge of the derivation.
  2. Recieve a worksheet with formulaic, boring problems
  3. Apply the formula
  4. Turn in the work
  5. Recieve critique
  6. Do better next time (or not)
